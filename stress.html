Understanding Stress

Symptoms of Stress

Stress can manifest physically, emotionally, and behaviorally. Some common symptoms include:

  • Headaches and muscle tension
  • Fatigue and difficulty sleeping
  • Increased irritability and mood swings
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Feeling overwhelmed or anxious
  • Changes in appetite (eating more or less)

Causes of Stress

Stress can be caused by a variety of factors â€” sometimes it builds up from daily pressures, and sometimes it strikes suddenly. Common causes include:

  • Work-related pressures or job loss
  • Financial struggles
  • Relationship conflicts or breakups
  • Chronic illness or health problems
  • Major life changes (moving, divorce, loss of a loved one)
  • Academic pressure or workload

How to Help Yourself

Managing stress starts with understanding your own body and reactions. Here are ways you can help yourself:

  • Practice relaxation techniques: deep breathing, meditation, or yoga can help calm your mind.
  • Exercise regularly: even a 20-minute walk can reduce stress hormones.
  • Stay organized: prioritize tasks to avoid feeling overwhelmed.
  • Maintain healthy boundaries: learn to say "no" when needed.
  • Eat healthy and sleep well: your body needs energy and rest to cope with stress.
  • Talk it out: sharing your feelings with a trusted friend or therapist can make a huge difference.

How to Help Someone Else

Supporting someone under stress can be life-changing for them. Here's how you can help:

  • Be a good listener: sometimes, just being heard is enough to ease someone's burden.
  • Avoid offering quick fixes: instead, validate their feelings and let them express themselves.
  • Encourage healthy habits: suggest going for a walk together or doing a calming activity.
  • Offer practical help: if they're overwhelmed, offer to help with tasks or errands.
  • Suggest professional support: remind them that it's okay to seek counseling or therapy.
